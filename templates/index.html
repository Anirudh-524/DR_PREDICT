Process
From image to diagnosis
in three steps
The pipeline is fully automated — upload a standard fundus photograph and receive a graded result with visual explanation within seconds.
01
🖼️
Image ingestion
A fundus photograph is uploaded and preprocessed — resized to 224×224, normalised, and optionally enhanced with CLAHE for improved vessel contrast.
02
🧠
Hybrid feature extraction
EfficientNet extracts local texture and lesion features while the Vision Transformer captures global spatial relationships across the retinal field simultaneously.
03
📊
Graded classification
Fused features pass through the classification head, producing per-class confidence scores and a Grad-CAM heatmap highlighting the regions that drove the prediction.
Classification
Five severity grades
Based on the international ETDRS grading scale for diabetic retinopathy severity.
Grade 0
No DR
Normal retina with no signs of diabetic change. Healthy vasculature and optic disc.
Grade 1
Mild
Microaneurysms only — small outpouchings in retinal capillaries, earliest sign of NPDR.
Grade 2
Moderate
Dot haemorrhages, hard exudates, and cotton-wool spots indicating broader vascular damage.
Grade 3
Severe
Extensive haemorrhages in all quadrants, venous beading, and intraretinal microvascular abnormalities.
Grade 4
Proliferative DR
Neovascularisation with high risk of vitreous haemorrhage and tractional retinal detachment.
